The word apple, whose Old English ancestor is æppel, is descended
from the Proto-Germanic noun *aplaz, descended in turn from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ébōl.[5] As late as the 17th century, the word also functioned as a generic term for all fruit, including nuts. This can be compared to the 14th-century Middle English expression appel of paradis, meaning a banana.[6]
